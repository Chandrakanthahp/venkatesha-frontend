Micro ear surgery
Micro ear surgery, or microscopic otologic surgery, uses high-powered microscopes and tiny instruments for precise treatment of complex ear issues like hearing loss, infections, tumors, and structural defects, involving procedures like tympanoplasty (eardrum repair) or stapedotomy (for fixed stapes bone), aiming to restore hearing and function with minimally invasive techniques for faster recovery.
Endoscopic ear surgery
Endoscopic ear surgery (EES) is a minimally invasive technique using a tiny camera (endoscope) to view and operate inside the middle ear, offering a wider, magnified view than traditional microscopy, especially in tight spaces like the ear canal. It avoids the large incision behind the ear, leading to less pain, faster recovery, and better cosmetic results, treating conditions like cholesteatoma, tympanic membrane perforations, and ossicular chain problems with similar success rates to older methods, and is becoming a preferred approach for many ear surgeries.

Ossiculoplasty
Ossiculoplasty is a middle ear surgery to repair or rebuild the tiny hearing bones (ossicles: malleus, incus, stapes) damaged by infection or trauma, restoring the sound conduction pathway and improving conductive hearing loss.
Stapedectomy
A stapedectomy is a microsurgery to treat conductive hearing loss, usually from otosclerosis, where the tiny stapes bone in the middle ear becomes fixed, stopping sound vibration; the surgeon removes the stapes (or part of it) and replaces it with a prosthetic device (prosthesis) to restore sound conduction to the inner ear, offering high success rates for hearing improvement.
